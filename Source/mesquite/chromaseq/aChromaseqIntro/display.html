Display Options

In the Chromatogram window, the following display options are available from the Chromatograms menu:

Show Control Bar: toggles the control bar immediately to the left of the chromatogram traces. There are three components to the control bar:

Hide chromatogram – Clicking on this triangle will hide the chromatogram to the right of the triangle. The base calls will still be visable.
Show chromatogram – Clicking on this triangle will show the hidden chromatogram to the right of the triangle.
Forward view – a blue-boxed arrow pointing to the right indicates that all the chromatograms/sequences are shown in their forward direction. Clicking on the arrow will reverse the chromatograms and corresponding sequences.
Reverse view – a yellow-boxed arrow pointing to the left indicates that the chromatograms/sequences are shown in their reverse direction. Clicking on the arrow will return the chromatograms and corresponding sequences to the forward direction.
Complement off – a blue-boxed "C" indicates that the complements of chromatograms/sequences are not shown. Clicking on the "C" will complement the chromatograms and corresonding sequences.
Complement on – a yellow-boxed "C" indicates that the complements of the chromatograms/sequences are shown. Clicking on the "C" will return the chromatograms and corresponding sequences to their normal (non-complement) display.


Show Matrix: Selecting this item will bring the corresponding Character Matrix window to the front. If the window is not open, Mesquite will open the window, then bring it to the front.

Color By Quality: Toggles weather the base calls are shaded according to phred/phrap quality scores. High quality scores are paler (white), while low quality base calls are darker (green or blue). Those sites which Mesquite has called ambiguous are colored grey.

Thick Trace Lines: Toggles the thickness of the chromatogram trace lines.

Fade Low Quality

The following options for fading reads are available from the Chromatograms menu:

Show Fades: toggles the brightness of peaks based on quality criteria (below).

Fade Low Quality: low quality peaks are faded, if "Show Fades" is checked. Note, after checking Fade Low Quality, if the low quality peaks are not faded, you mush activate fading by selecting Chromatograms > Show Fades. You can set the quality threshold at which peaks are faded at Chromatograms > Fade Quality Threshold….

Fade Non-Source Read: sets whether reads are dimmed in regions of where they are not the source.

Fade Non-Source Lower-Quality Read: sets whether reads are dimmed in regions of where they are not the source AND of lower quality than the source

Conflict Markers

Mesquite can highlight conflicts among reads and sequences

Show Source Read Conflicts with Higher Quality Read: sets whether to draw a box around the Phred calls for which the a lower quality read is the source and this conflicts with a higher quality read.

Show Read-Matrix Conflict: sets whether shading indicates whether a chromatogram's call is different from matrix state

Show Read-Read Conflict: sets whether bar indicates differences among the Phred calls for each read. These conflicts are shown as red boxes under the corresponding base calls in the Phred.Phrap.Mesquite listing (usually the third sequence from the bottom of the window).

Dim Conflict Markers if Low Quality: Sets whether the red boxes for low quality conflicts are dimmed below the bases in the Phred.Phrap.Mesquite listing. The degree to which the red box is dimmed is based on the amount of conflict between reads. When a high-quality base call conflicts with a low-quality base call, the box is paler; however, when the low-quality conflict has a higher quality base call (i.e. a relatively high, single peak), the red box will be brighter.
The threshold for dimming conflicts can be set at Chromatograms > Dim Conflict Marker Threshold…; the threshold is the quality score of the conflicting (low-quality) base call.
