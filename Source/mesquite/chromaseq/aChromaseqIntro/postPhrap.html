Post-Phrap Options

 

Mesquite can perform some additional manipulations of sequences, after Phred and Phrap have finished processing (most of these manipulations are based on quality scores). However, the default settings should be appropriate for most users.

  • Quality threshold for lower case: Mesquite will change upper case nucleotides to lower case if the quality score falls below the specified quality threshold.
  • Convert multiple-peaks to ambiguity codes: if multiple peaks are present at a site, Mesquite will convert the base call to reflect this ambiguity, based on the relative signal strength of the secondary peak(s). In the default settings, secondary peaks that are at least 30% as high as the tallest peak at a site are counted towards the abiguity at that site.
  • Trim low quality regions from ends (FASTA & import): Mesquite will also trim low quality ends of sequences, if you so choose. This trimming is done by removing regions of leading or trailing nucleotides with quality values less than the specified quality threshold. If the ends have a mixture of lower and higher quality nucleotides, then Mesquite will trim until if finds a window of nucleotides of specified length which have fewer than the "Trim window threshold" of nucleotides below the quality score. To put it another way, it will trim leading and trailing windows of nucleotides which have as many or more poor-quality nucleotides than specified; it trims high-quality nucleotides only if they are external to low-quality nucleotides that are to be trimmed by this criterion.
  • rename contigs in .ace files: renames the contigs in the modified .ace files based on the naming rules. Two ace files are actually produced, but only one will show the naming modifications (files that end with "m.ace").
  • add fragment name to contig name: adds a string corresponding to the name of the fragment (gene) to the contig in the m.ace file. The previous option (rename contigs) must be selected for the fragment name to be appended to the contig name.
  • open directory with fasta files: opens the directory in which the individual FASTA files are stored (this directory is usually named "processedFasta").
